Proof of Theorem 5
Roots of Unity in the Integers Modulo p
Statement

In the integers modulo $p$, where $p$ is any prime number, for all $0 < a < p$, $a$ is a $(p - 1)$-th root of unity.

Also, there's at least one number $0 < b < p$ such that $b$ is a primitive $(p - 1)$-th root of unity.
