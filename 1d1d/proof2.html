Proof: The Quadrangle Inequality Implies the Turning Point Property

Recall that the Turning Point Property can be formulated as follows: \[ \forall j \lt j', \nexists i \lt i': \\ \begin{align} dp[j'] + f(i, j') & \gt dp[j] + f(i, j) \\ dp[j] + f(i', j) & \gt dp[j'] + f(i', j) \end{align} \] and that the Quandrangle Inequality is satisfied if \( \forall j \lt j' \lt i \lt i', f(i, j) + f(i', j') \geq f(i', j) + f(i, j') \).

For the sake of contradiction, assume the negation: \[ \exists j \lt j', \exists i \lt i': \\ \begin{align} dp[j'] + f(i, j') & \gt dp[j] + f(i, j) \\ dp[j] + f(i', j) & \gt dp[j'] + f(i', j) \end{align} \] Adding the inequalities together, \[ \require{cancel} \begin{align} dp[j'] + f(i, j') + dp[j] + f(i', j) & \gt dp[j] + f(i, j) + dp[j'] + f(i', j) \\ \cancel{dp[j']} + f(i, j') + \cancel{dp[j]} + f(i', j) & \gt \cancel{dp[j]} + f(i, j) + \cancel{dp[j']} + f(i', j) \\ f(i, j') + f(i', j) & \gt f(i, j) + f(i', j) \\ f(i, j) + f(i', j) & \lt f(i, j') + f(i', j) \end{align} \] This is a contradiction of the Quadrangle Inequality, which states that \( \forall j \lt j' \lt i \lt i', f(i, j) + f(i', j') \geq f(i', j) + f(i, j') \).

There is a contradiction in the negation, so the proof is completed.\( \ \square \)
